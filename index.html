Methodology

Over the course of a year, from 1/20/15 to 1/20/16, every instance of defecation was recorded in a spreadsheet. Defecation, I defined as, a sit, a loaf, a wipe, and a flush. Courtusy flushes, half an hour slugfests, and multiple wipe and poo sessions did not increase the tally more than once. I recorded four statistics regarding each poop, date, time, location, and consistancy. Consistancy was measured via eyeball, in reference to the Bristol School Scale . Raw data can be found here. Total Poops and Average Consistancy
2a - I averaged 42 poops per month, with a high of 53 (January) and a low of 35 (August). The inconsistancies, taking into account the differences of days in a month, I found expected. The average consistancy also varied wildly, with a high of 4.4 (October was a sloppy month), and a low of 3.5 in May. The only trend that seemed to hold true was a higher volume of poops during colder months. I attribute this to more leafy greens and fresh fruit consumed during summer, and more hearty meals and more indoor time during the winter.
2b - Here we really start to see a trend forming, a skyrocket in the looseness of my stool on Sunday correlates to my habit of having more than a few drinks on Saturday nights. Poops are fairly consistant during the week, most likely due to perdictable coffee consumption. We also notice a slight droop during Friday and the weekend, when I generally have better things to do than poop!
